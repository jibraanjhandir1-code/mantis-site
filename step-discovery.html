Phase One — Discovery & Audit

We need to understand your operation before we touch anything.

Most automation projects fail because someone built a system around a process nobody fully understood. We spend the first phase making sure that doesn't happen.

What Actually Happens

It starts with a working session, not a sales call.

The first thing we ask is simple: walk us through what you actually do. Not a high-level summary — the real thing. Where do you open your email, what do you copy, where do you paste it, what do you check next. We want to see the spreadsheet you've been maintaining for three years and the Slack message you send every Friday at 4pm that could be automated in a week.

Most clients come in thinking they know which parts of their operation need automation. After the audit, the list almost always looks different. The things that feel painful often have quick fixes. The things that feel normal are sometimes costing thousands of hours a year.

We map it all before we prioritize anything.

Every manual step, every tool, every handoff — we document the full picture first. Then we sit down and sort it by two things: how much time it costs you, and how straightforward it is to automate. That intersection is where we start.

We're also looking for things that aren't obviously broken but create compounding problems downstream. A data entry step that takes two minutes might be causing four hours of cleanup elsewhere. You don't always see that until someone draws the whole map.

The ROI projection is built from your actual numbers.

At the end of this phase, we put together a document that shows exactly what we found, what we'd build first, and what the return looks like in real terms — hours saved per week, cost reduction, error rate improvement. No made-up percentages. Everything is calculated from what you told us and what we observed.

You can take that document and do nothing with it. Most people don't.
